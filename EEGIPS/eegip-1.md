## Abstract

This document serves as the inaugural Ethereum Event Gardeners Improvement Proposal (EEGIP). It outlines the mission, objectives, and governance structure of the Ethereum Event Gardeners and serves as a guideline for future EEGIPs.

## Motivation

In the expansive ecosystem of Ethereum, there exists a need for a coordinated, community-driven effort to organize, enhance, and diversify events. This EEGIP aims to establish a formal process to propose and enact initiatives that contribute to this vision.

## Specification

### Mission

To cultivate a vibrant, inclusive, and diverse Ethereum community through events, gatherings, and education.

### Objectives

1. Promote decentralization in event planning and execution.
2. Foster a culture of inclusivity and diversity.
3. Encourage open participation and collaboration.

### Governance Structure

1. **Proposal Submission**: Any community member can submit an EEGIP.
2. **Review**: Initial review by a committee to assess the feasibility and alignment with mission and objectives.
3. **Community Discussion**: Open forum for community members to debate and refine the proposal.
4. **Voting**: Final approval through community vote or consensus.

### EEGIP Format

1. **Abstract**: Brief summary of the proposal.
2. **Motivation**: Rationale behind the proposal.
3. **Specification**: Detailed description, including any technical requirements.
4. **Implementation**: Steps for executing the proposal.

### EEGIP Lifecycle

1. **Draft**: Initial proposal state.
2. **Discussion**: Open for community debate.
3. **Accepted**: Approved and ready for implementation.
4. **Implemented**: Successfully carried out.
5. **Deferred/Rejected**: Not approved or postponed for later reconsideration.

## Implementation

This proposal will be considered accepted once it receives approval from a majority of the Ethereum Event Gardeners community members and steering committee. Future EEGIPs will follow the framework established herein.
